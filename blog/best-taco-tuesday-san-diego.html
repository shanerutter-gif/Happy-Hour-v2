Best Taco Tuesday Deals in San Diego (2026)

Half-off tacos, $2 street tacos, and all-you-can-eat specials that actually slap — every verified Taco Tuesday deal worth your Tuesday night in San Diego.

San Diego takes Taco Tuesday seriously. The city has more good cheap taco deals on a Tuesday than most cities manage all week — and the best Taco Tuesday deals in San Diego in 2026 are genuinely competitive, stacked alongside happy hour pricing to stretch your dollar further. You just need to know which spots are worth the trip and which ones are coasting on the name.

Best Taco Tuesday Deals in the Gaslamp Quarter

The Gaslamp is where most people end up for Taco Tuesday, and the competition down 5th Ave means the deals are genuinely good. Three spots in particular are worth building a Tuesday night around.

El Chingon — The Premium Play

Half-off tacos all day Tuesday · AYCE tacos 4–7:42pm with Don Julio 1942 bottle

El Chingon at 560 Fifth Ave runs one of the most theatrical Taco Tuesday setups in the city. The "FORTYTWOSDAYS" promotion — named for the 7:42pm cut-off — lets a table of four get two hours of unlimited tacos when someone orders a bottle of Don Julio 1942. That's a bottle-service setup dressed as a taco deal, and for a group splitting the cost it can work out to strong value. On top of that, tacos are half-off all day on Tuesdays, which means the standard menu is accessible even if you're not buying a premium bottle. The La Completa Taco Flight — carne asada, pollo asada, al pastor, carnitas, and a pescado taco — is the move for a solo visit. Open 11am–2am daily, so you can get there early or late.

American Junkie — The Best Value

All-you-can-eat tacos 5–10pm with any drink purchase

If the question is maximum tacos per dollar, American Junkie at 628 5th Ave wins. AYCE tacos from 5pm to 10pm with any drink purchase — a $6 Cazadores or $4 draft Pacifico qualifies — is an exceptional deal by any measure. The space is a loud, high-energy bar that transitions to one of the Gaslamp's busiest late-night venues after 10pm, so Taco Tuesday here starts with dinner and can turn into a full night out without moving. Happy hour runs from 5–8pm on top of the taco deal, so you can layer cheap drinks onto AYCE food for several overlapping hours. Come with a group.

Barleymash — The Pre-Taco Drinks Spot

Half-off all beer, wine, spirits & cocktails daily 4–6pm · 30 SD craft beers on tap

Barleymash at 600 5th Ave isn't specifically a taco spot, but it's the best happy hour bar on the block and a natural first stop before walking to El Chingon or American Junkie next door. Half off everything — not just a handful of specials, but the whole bar — during their 4–6pm window, with 30 San Diego craft beers rotating on tap. A pint of something local before a AYCE taco run is a reasonable way to start a Tuesday. The brunch is also genuinely good if you're doing a weekend version of this plan.

Best Taco Tuesday Outside the Gaslamp

The Gaslamp concentrates a lot of the city's Taco Tuesday energy, but the best pure street taco deal in San Diego is actually a few miles north.

La Puerta — Mission Hills

$2 street tacos all day Tuesday · $2 TJ Dawgs · $3 Tecate · Happy hour 3–7pm daily

La Puerta on Goldfinch St in Mission Hills runs the most generous all-day Taco Tuesday in the city. Two-dollar street tacos from 3pm to close, $2 TJ-style hot dogs, and $3 Tecate — and that's before the 3–7pm happy hour kicks in, which adds half-off all drinks and appetizers on top. The tequila program is serious: over 100 labels, with a Sinaloa-inspired Mexican menu to match. This is the spot for a long, unhurried Tuesday with no pressure to spend up. Not in the Gaslamp so it draws a slightly calmer crowd than the Fifth Ave strip, which can be a feature depending on your Tuesday energy. Find it on Spotd.

Best Order at Each Spot

Knowing what to order at each spot is the difference between a good Taco Tuesday and a great one. Here's what the regulars get:

El Chingon

The La Completa Taco Flight — five tacos (carne asada, pollo asada, al pastor, carnitas, pescado) at half-off pricing. If you're going with a group of four and considering the bottle promotion, the Don Julio 1942 AYCE deal works out to roughly $30–40 per person all-in, which is remarkable for premium tequila and unlimited tacos. The guacamole and chips at $3 off during happy hour is the right start.

American Junkie

The tacos are straightforward — seasoned ground beef or chicken, toppings bar, flour or corn tortilla. The play here is volume, not complexity. Order a $6 Cazadores margarita to qualify for the AYCE deal, eat tacos until you're done, then stay for the nightlife. The draft Pacifico ($4) is the economical qualifier if you want to keep the drink spend low.

Barleymash

This is a drinks stop, not a taco stop. The SD craft beer of the week at half off is the move — whatever local brewery they're rotating, it's going to be better than a standard Gaslamp beer at a better price. Ask the bartender what's on tap and let them suggest. 30 options means there's something for every preference.

La Puerta

The al pastor street taco at $2 is the default — it's the taco that made La Puerta a Mission Hills institution. Get four. Add a TJ-style hot dog ($2) if you want something different. Wash it down with a $3 Tecate and you've spent $15 on a complete Tuesday night. The tequila program (100+ labels) is there if you want to go deeper — ask for a mezcal or a Jalisco blanco to round things out.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Taco Tuesday in San Diego

Combine happy hour with Taco Tuesday. Most of these spots run overlapping deals — the sweet spot is arriving between 4–6pm, when happy hour pricing and Taco Tuesday specials stack. At American Junkie, for instance, both the $4 drafts and the AYCE taco deal run simultaneously between 5–6pm.

The Gaslamp fills up fast on Tuesdays. El Chingon and American Junkie in particular get packed by 6pm in summer. If you're going with a group, arrive by 5:30 or put your name in early. The side streets off 5th Ave have parking after 6pm that most tourists miss.

La Puerta is better for a relaxed Tuesday. If you want $2 tacos and a proper tequila without the Gaslamp energy, the Mission Hills location is the call. The crowd skews local, the patio is good, and the 100+ tequila list is the best reason to go slowly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Taco Tuesday deal in San Diego?

American Junkie at 628 5th Ave in the Gaslamp offers all-you-can-eat tacos from 5–10pm with any drink purchase — making it the best pure value Taco Tuesday deal in San Diego. El Chingon runs half-off tacos all day plus AYCE with a bottle purchase.

Where are the best Taco Tuesday deals in the Gaslamp?

The best Gaslamp Taco Tuesday spots are El Chingon (560 Fifth Ave, half-off tacos all day + AYCE promotion), American Junkie (628 5th Ave, AYCE tacos 5–10pm with any drink), and Barleymash (600 5th Ave, half-off everything 4–6pm as a pre-taco drinks stop).

How much do tacos cost on Taco Tuesday in San Diego?

Taco Tuesday prices in San Diego range from $2 street tacos at La Puerta (Mission Hills) to half-off menu tacos at El Chingon. American Junkie's AYCE deal starts at the price of a single drink (~$4–6).

Are there all-day Taco Tuesday deals in San Diego?

Yes. El Chingon runs half-off tacos all day Tuesday (not just during happy hour). La Puerta in Mission Hills runs $2 street tacos from 3pm to close on Tuesdays.
