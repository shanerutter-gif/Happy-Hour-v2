Best Happy Hours in Costa Mesa (2026)

$2 margaritas at Playa Mesa, all-night Latin tapas at Cafe Sevilla, honest Irish pints at Durty Nelly's — Costa Mesa is Orange County's most underrated happy hour neighborhood and it's not close.

By Emma June 8, 2026 9 min read
Share

Newport Beach gets the press. Laguna Beach gets the postcards. Costa Mesa gets the deals. If you've been sleeping on Costa Mesa's best happy hours, you've been paying too much for drinks in Orange County — and that's a problem worth solving tonight.

Costa Mesa doesn't have the beachfront views of its neighbors to the south, but it has something more useful: a dense, walkable grid of bars and restaurants that compete on quality and price instead of geography. The South Coast Metro and 17th Street corridors, Triangle Square, and the East Side neighborhoods have quietly assembled one of the best-value happy hour lineups in OC. Best Happy Hours in Costa Mesa: The Verified List

Playa Mesa — Best Value Happy Hour

Happy hour Monday–Friday, 3–6pm — $2 off margaritas, $5 tacos, $4 beers.

Playa Mesa at 428 E 17th St is the most consistent happy hour value in Costa Mesa. The Mexican kitchen and cocktail bar concept works well in this format: margaritas that are $12–14 at full price become genuinely affordable at $2 off, the taco pricing is already reasonable at $5, and the beer list includes a rotation of Mexican imports and craft options at $4. The 17th Street location is in the East Side neighborhood, which is more residential and local-feeling than the Triangle Square strip — the crowd here is regulars who live nearby, not tourists doing a lap of OC dining.

The outdoor patio is worth requesting on weekdays before crowds build. The margarita menu rotates seasonally, but the house margarita — blanco tequila, fresh lime, agave — is what you're there for. This is the rare happy hour where the food is as good as the drinks and both are genuinely priced to move. Monday through Friday, 3–6pm. No reservations needed for bar and patio seating.

Cafe Sevilla — Best Atmosphere

Happy hour Wednesday–Sunday, 5–7pm — 50% off all tapas, $8 sangrias, $6 craft beers, $9 cocktails. Live music nightly.

There's no Costa Mesa happy hour list worth reading that doesn't include Cafe Sevilla at 1870 Harbor Blvd in Triangle Square. The Spanish restaurant and tapas bar has been running one of Orange County's most distinctive happy hours for years, and the combination of 50% off a proper tapas menu plus live Latin music every night of the week makes it legitimately hard to match on atmosphere alone. The sangria is house-made and good at $8; the cocktail list is genuine Spanish-influenced work at $9.

The 50% off tapas applies to the full tapas menu — which means patatas bravas, gambas al ajillo, pan con tomate, jamón serrano boards, and a rotating selection of seasonal bites all at half price. The dining room has a warm, cave-like interior with tile and candles; the bar fills quickly on Fridays and Saturdays. Note that Cafe Sevilla is closed Monday and Tuesday — Wednesday through Sunday only, with Sunday happy hour starting at 3:30pm (the restaurant opens earlier on Sundays). The late-night element — live music continues well past happy hour — makes this the best spot on the list if you're planning a full evening rather than a quick drink.

Durty Nelly's — Best Beer Selection

Happy hour Monday–Thursday, 5–7pm — $3 domestic bottles, $4 imported beers, $4–$8 food menu.

Every neighborhood needs a reliable Irish pub and Costa Mesa's is Durty Nelly's at 2915 Red Hill Ave. The Red Hill location is slightly off the main strip, which keeps it genuinely local in a way that the Harbor Blvd corridor spots can't always claim. The happy hour here is simple and honest: $3 domestic bottles, $4 imports, $4–$8 food. The beer list includes the expected Irish and European imports alongside a solid American domestic selection — this is not a craft beer bar but it doesn't pretend to be, and the pricing reflects that clarity.

The food menu during happy hour includes pub staples — wings, sliders, loaded fries, a few Irish-specific items — at pricing that makes a few plates and a couple of rounds a very inexpensive weekday evening. The trivia nights (check the weekly schedule) draw a regular crowd mid-week and are some of the best-attended pub trivia events in Costa Mesa. Monday through Thursday, 5–7pm; the bar is open past happy hour and remains a solid neighborhood option through the week.

The Country Club — Best Cocktails

Happy hour Wednesday–Friday, 4–6:30pm — $7 wine, $6 draft beers, $8 cocktails, half-off select appetizers.

The Country Club at 330 E 17th St is the most polished bar experience on this list — a chef-driven American restaurant with a cocktail program that takes itself seriously, a smart menu of prime steaks and handmade pasta, and a bar that transitions seamlessly between happy hour and the dinner crowd. The happy hour pricing is well-calibrated: $8 cocktails on a menu where full-price drinks regularly run $14–16 is a meaningful discount, and the half-off appetizers apply to plates that are genuinely kitchen-quality (not bar snacks). The wine list at $7 is a step above the usual pour, and the draft selection at $6 covers a rotating set of California craft options.

Wednesday through Friday, 4–6:30pm. The Country Club is closed Monday and Tuesday. This is the spot to bring someone who would usually flinch at a "happy hour" label — the room is upscale without being pretentious, the deals are real, and the transition to full dinner service is seamless if you decide to stay. The outdoor patio on 17th Street is worth requesting in the summer months.

Palenque Kitchen — Best Mezcal & Tequila

Happy hour Monday–Thursday, 5–6:30pm — $8 mezcal cocktails, $6 tequila shots, $5 beers, half-off appetizers.

If there's one hidden gem on this list, it's Palenque Kitchen at 1749 Newport Blvd. The upscale Mexican kitchen with a specialty mezcal and tequila bar has one of the most interesting spirit lists in Costa Mesa — the bar program is genuinely built around agave spirits in a way that most Mexican restaurants in OC are not — and the happy hour prices make it accessible to explore. $8 mezcal cocktails is a compelling entry point for anyone curious about the category; the house selection includes both entry-level bottles and a few more serious expressions. The $6 tequila shot pricing covers a curated short-list of quality blancos and reposados that are worth ordering straight.

The food menu during happy hour extends to half-off appetizers from a kitchen that does proper Mexican cooking: ceviche, guacamole, tacos al pastor, elote. The Newport Blvd location is walkable from the 17th Street strip. Monday through Thursday, 5–6:30pm — the shortest window on this list, so arrive with a plan. The patio and bar seats are first-come on weeknights.

How to Plan a Costa Mesa Happy Hour Crawl

The geography of Costa Mesa's best happy hours makes a crawl genuinely viable on foot or with a short rideshare hop. Playa Mesa and The Country Club are both on or near 17th Street — the East Side's main dining corridor — about a 5-minute walk from each other. Palenque Kitchen is about a 10-minute walk north on Newport Blvd. Cafe Sevilla and the Triangle Square cluster are about 2 miles west on Harbor Blvd, best reached via a short rideshare.

A practical Tuesday through Thursday route: Playa Mesa from 3–4pm for tacos and a margarita, Palenque Kitchen from 5–6:30pm for mezcal cocktails and guacamole, then Durty Nelly's for a pint before the kitchen stops taking orders. On a Wednesday through Friday, you can add Cafe Sevilla later in the evening for tapas and live music — the kitchen stays open well past happy hour and the music starts around 8pm. The Country Club works well as either the first cocktail of the evening or a proper dinner destination after the other spots.

One practical note: parking in Costa Mesa is generally easier than Newport Beach or Laguna Beach, and there are free lots behind most of the 17th Street restaurants. If you're doing Triangle Square, the on-site parking structure is free with restaurant validation.

Costa Mesa Happy Hours vs. Newport Beach and Laguna

The honest case for Costa Mesa: lower prices, better food-to-drink ratios, less tourist markup, and a crowd that's actually local. A $5 taco at Playa Mesa would be a $9 taco in Newport Beach. A $3 pint at Durty Nelly's is a rare find anywhere on the coast. Even Cafe Sevilla's $8 sangria and 50% off tapas represents a price point that's genuinely hard to replicate in Laguna Beach or Balboa Island.

The tradeoff is obvious: you won't be drinking with an ocean view at Playa Mesa. But for a Monday through Thursday happy hour when you're prioritizing quality and value over scenery, Costa Mesa consistently outperforms its better-known neighbors. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best happy hour in Costa Mesa?
The best overall happy hour in Costa Mesa for value and variety is Playa Mesa — $2 off margaritas, $5 tacos, and $4 beers running Monday through Friday 3–6pm. For atmosphere and food quality, Cafe Sevilla's 50% off tapas and $8 sangrias on Wednesday through Sunday 5–7pm is the best experience in the neighborhood.
Which Costa Mesa bars have happy hour on weekdays?
Playa Mesa (Mon–Fri 3–6pm), Durty Nelly's (Mon–Thu 5–7pm), and Palenque Kitchen (Mon–Thu 5–6:30pm) all run weekday happy hours including Mondays. Cafe Sevilla and The Country Club run happy hour Wednesday through Friday. Most Costa Mesa happy hours fall in the 3–7pm window on weekdays.
Where is the best happy hour near Triangle Square in Costa Mesa?
Cafe Sevilla is located directly inside Triangle Square at 1870 Harbor Blvd and is the best happy hour in the complex — 50% off tapas, $8 sangrias, live Latin music, running Wednesday through Sunday 5–7pm. Other nearby options include Playa Mesa (0.3 miles east) and The Country Club (0.2 miles south on 17th Street).
Does Costa Mesa have good happy hours compared to Newport Beach or Laguna Beach?
Yes — Costa Mesa actually has some of the best price-to-quality happy hours in Orange County. Compared to Newport Beach and Laguna Beach, Costa Mesa prices are noticeably lower while the food and drink quality stays high. You can get $5 tacos at Playa Mesa, $3 pints at Durty Nelly's, and $8 mezcal cocktails at Palenque Kitchen — price points that are rare in the more tourist-facing beach cities.
