Best Trivia Nights in San Diego — Every Day of the Week

Whether you're a Tuesday regular or a weekend warrior, here's where to flex your brain (and score free drinks).

By Ryan March 24, 2026 7 min read
Share
📋 In this article

    Trivia night is having a moment in San Diego. What started as a Tuesday-night pub quiz tradition has exploded into a 7-days-a-week scene with themed rounds, cash prizes, and some seriously competitive regulars.

    We mapped out the best trivia nights for every day of the week across San Diego — from laid-back neighborhood joints to high-energy brewery showdowns. Whether you're looking for a casual hang or want to dominate the leaderboard, there's a night (and a style) for you.

    What Makes a Great Trivia Night?

    Not all trivia is created equal. The best nights nail three things: a good host who keeps energy high, fair questions that balance pop culture and actual knowledge, and real prizes worth playing for. Bonus points for drink specials tied to the event.

    Monday

    Kick Off the Week with Brainpower

    Monday trivia is the underdog of the week — smaller crowds, easier to grab a table, and often the most relaxed vibe. Several North Park bars run Monday nights as their flagship trivia event, knowing the competition is lighter.

    What to expect: General knowledge formats, 4-6 rounds, and bar tab prizes ranging from $25-50. Most Monday nights start at 7 PM and wrap by 9 PM — perfect for a school night.

    Tuesday

    The OG Trivia Night

    Tuesday is the classic trivia night. It's the most popular day nationally, and San Diego is no exception. You'll find the widest variety of hosts, formats, and venues on Tuesday. The trade-off? Bigger crowds and stiffer competition.

    Pro tip: Arrive 30-45 minutes early for popular Tuesday nights. The best spots fill up fast, and some have team size limits (usually 6 max).

    Best neighborhoods: North Park, Hillcrest, and the Gaslamp all have multiple Tuesday trivia options within walking distance of each other.

    Wednesday

    Hump Day Brain Break

    Wednesday trivia tends to attract the most dedicated players. The crowds are slightly smaller than Tuesday, but the regulars are serious. Many Wednesday nights feature themed rounds — music, movies, science, or sports — mixed in with general knowledge.

    Best for: Teams who want a challenge. Wednesday regulars tend to be experienced and competitive. Bring your A-game.

    Thursday

    The Pre-Weekend Warm-Up

    Thursday trivia has a different energy — looser, louder, and more social. Bars often pair trivia with extended happy hours, making it a natural transition into the weekend. Several breweries in Miramar and North Park run Thursday nights with $4-5 pint specials alongside trivia.

    Best move: Find a spot that does both trivia and happy hour so you're saving money while earning prizes. Several Spotd-listed venues offer this combo.

    Friday

    Date Night Trivia

    Friday trivia is rare but growing. The spots that run it lean into the date-night crowd with couples-friendly formats, cocktail specials, and a more upscale atmosphere. Little Italy and East Village have the best Friday options.

    Format: Often shorter (3-4 rounds) with a faster pace. Some venues do "speed trivia" where rounds are timed at 30 seconds per question.

    Saturday

    Weekend Trivia for the Committed

    Saturday trivia is niche but loyal. The venues that host it tend to be breweries and taprooms that draw afternoon crowds. Start times are earlier (3-5 PM), and the vibe is casual — think day-drinking energy with a side of brain teasers.

    Best for: Groups looking for a daytime activity that isn't brunch. Combine with a brewery crawl in Miramar or a stroll through North Park.

    Sunday

    Sunday Funday Brain Edition

    Sunday trivia has exploded in San Diego. It pairs perfectly with the city's existing Sunday Funday culture — brunch transitions into trivia, which transitions into watching the sunset. Several bars in Pacific Beach and Ocean Beach run Sunday evening trivia with solid attendance.

    NFL season bonus: During football season (September-February), many sports bars run football-themed trivia before or after games. Great energy, bigger crowds, and usually bigger prizes.

    Trivia Night Survival Guide

    • Team size: 4-5 people is the sweet spot. Enough brain variety without too many cooks.
    • Specialization matters: Build a team with different strengths — sports, science, pop culture, history, music. The best teams have breadth.
    • Phone policy: Most hosts are strict about no phones during rounds. Don't be that team.
    • Arrive early: 30 minutes before start for popular nights. Some spots take reservations for trivia — always ask.
    • Tip your host: Good trivia hosts make the night. Most work for tips and a portion of the bar's take. Show appreciation.
