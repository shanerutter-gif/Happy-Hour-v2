    Why Hillcrest Has Some of San Diego's Best Happy Hours

    Hillcrest earns its reputation as one of San Diego's most liveable urban neighbourhoods, and the bar and restaurant scene reflects it. A few things set it apart from other city pockets:

    • Genuine community character. Hillcrest is San Diego's historically LGBTQ+ hub — the bar scene evolved over decades to serve a neighbourhood crowd rather than tourists, which keeps prices honest and atmospheres real. You'll find regulars at every spot on this list.
    • Early starts. Hillcrest Brewing Company and Baja Betty's both kick off at 2pm — earlier than almost anywhere else in the city. That matters for a weekday afternoon or a long Saturday.
    • Extended hours. Baja Betty's offers extended happy hour from 6pm to close, which is a genuinely rare perk in San Diego where most programmes cut off at 7pm.
    • Short walking distances. University Avenue from 5th to Normal Street is densely packed with options. A three-spot crawl might involve a 12-minute total walk.

    The Best Happy Hours in Hillcrest San Diego

    Hillcrest Brewing Company — Best Craft Beer Patio

    Happy hour daily 2–6pm — $2 off all draft beers, wines, ciders, High Noons, and well drinks. $3 pizza slices Mon–Fri. Hillcrest Brewing Company is the world's first out-and-proud gay brewery, and it's been a neighbourhood anchor on University Avenue since 2012. The happy hour is one of the longest and most consistent in the area: daily from 2pm to 6pm, covering everything from craft IPAs to canned hard seltzer to house wines. The covered patio is big enough to take a group, dog-friendly, and has multiple TVs for sport. 1458 University Ave.

    The $3 pizza slice deal during the week makes this a legitimate lunch-or-early-evening stop rather than just a drinks-only destination. Honey BBQ wings are a fan favourite alongside the draft pours.

    Baja Betty's — Best Margaritas (All Day + Night)

    Papi Hour daily 2–6pm + Extended Happy Hour 6pm to close — $6.75 house wine, sangria, Mi Casa margaritas, well drinks, and draft beers. Baja Betty's is famous for its tequila programme — over 100 tequilas — and the happy hour deal makes it an outrageously good-value entry point. The extended window (6pm to close) is the real differentiator: if you're going out for the evening, you can arrive any time after 2pm and drink at happy hour prices all night. The Mi Casa margarita at $6.75 is excellent. 1421 University Ave.

    The vibe is lively, the crowd is Hillcrest regulars, and the outdoor seating is good for people-watching on University. This is a genuinely reliable spot regardless of time of day or night.

    Common Stock — Best for a Nicer Round

    Happy hour weekdays — $6 select beers, $8 single-pour well spirits and house red/white wine by the glass, $9 featured cocktails. Common Stock on 5th Avenue is Hillcrest's casual-sophisticated option — not a dive bar, not a hotel lobby. The menu is New American with a strong brunch and dinner programme, and the happy hour drink list is a quality step up from the standard well-drink fare nearby. The $9 featured cocktail is usually rotating and worth ordering. 3805 5th Ave.

    The room has a warm, neighbourhood restaurant feel rather than a bar vibe, which makes it a good choice if you want a spot where conversation is easier. Full food menu available during happy hour, so you can turn a drink stop into a proper meal.

    Starlite — Best Cocktail Bar Happy Hour

    Happy hour daily 5–7pm — $5 draft beers, $5 well drinks, $2 off specialty cocktails. Starlite is technically in Middletown on India Street but is firmly in the Hillcrest-area orbit — a 10-minute walk from University Ave, and one of the few genuinely great cocktail bars in the neighbourhood. The $2 off specialty cocktails during happy hour drops $14–16 drinks to $12–14, which is how you get high-quality craft cocktails at something approaching a reasonable price in San Diego. 3175 India St.

    Starlite is 21+ at all times, so it skews slightly older and quieter than the University Ave spots. Late-night dining is available (open until 1am weekdays, 2am weekends), so this works as either a happy hour destination or a post-dinner cocktail stop. The bar staff is consistently excellent.

    Fort Oak — Best Food-Forward Happy Hour

    Happy hour Tue–Sun 4–6pm — $12 wood-fired grilled oysters, $12 crispy fried chicken wings, $10 classic cocktails (lemon drop, margarita, old fashioned). A Michelin Guide restaurant with a happy hour programme is not something San Diego does very often. Fort Oak in Mission Hills runs a tight weekday deal that makes its wood-fired kitchen accessible without the full tasting menu price tag. The $12 grilled oysters are the draw — they come from the wood-fired oven with a well-seasoned house mignonette and are genuinely excellent. 1011 Fort Stockton Dr, Mission Hills.

    Fort Oak also does dollar oyster night every Monday (outside the standard happy hour), which is worth knowing separately. Wednesday happy hour hours are 4–6pm. The room is sleek and the vibe is a notch above everything else on this list, which makes it the right choice if you're treating someone or closing out the week somewhere nicer.

    How to Do a Hillcrest Happy Hour Crawl

    Hillcrest's geography makes a crawl almost automatic. Here's the route locals use:

    2–3pm: Start at Hillcrest Brewing Company. Grab a $2 off pint and settle on the patio. If it's a weekday grab a $3 pizza slice with it. The 2pm start is genuinely rare in SD — use it.

    3–5pm: Move to Baja Betty's or Common Stock. Baja Betty's if you want a margarita and a livelier crowd. Common Stock if you want to sit down to something nicer and eat. Both are a short walk from the Brewing Company on University Ave.

    5–7pm: Starlite or Fort Oak for the evening cap. These are the spots for when you want the quality to go up a notch. Starlite's $5 drafts and $2-off cocktails are excellent for the money. Fort Oak's wood-fired oysters at $12 are the best food deal in the entire neighbourhood at that hour.

    Parking note: 5th Avenue and University Ave have metered street parking free after 6pm on weekdays and all day Sunday. The No. 10 and No. 11 bus lines connect Hillcrest directly from Downtown and Mission Valley. If you're coming from further afield, parking in the lot off Normal Street and walking is easier than fighting street spots on weekends.

    Weekday vs. weekend: Weekdays before 6pm are the best time to visit — you'll get seats at any spot on this list without planning ahead. Friday and Saturday evenings get busy, especially at Baja Betty's, so arriving before 7pm gives you a better experience at happy hour prices.

    What Else Hillcrest Has Going On

    Happy hour is the starting point. The Hillcrest and Uptown area has a full weekly calendar beyond it:

    • LGBTQ+ events: Hillcrest has active weekly programming at venues across the neighbourhood, from drag shows to queer trivia nights. The Spotd app surfaces these under Events when you enter the city.
    • Craft beer: Beyond Hillcrest Brewing, the area connects to North Park's craft beer corridor — Toronado, Mike Hess, and Half Door are within a 15-minute walk of University Ave.
    • Restaurant depth: Hillcrest's food scene runs from the Japanese omakase counter at Sushi Tadokoro to the legendary hash browns at Hash House A Go Go. The neighbourhood eats well in every direction at every price point.
    • Walkable nightlife: After 10pm the neighbourhood is active on University Ave, 5th, and around the normal street bars well past midnight most nights of the week.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What time is happy hour in Hillcrest San Diego?

    Hillcrest happy hours start as early as 2pm and run through the evening. Hillcrest Brewing Company and Baja Betty's both start at 2pm daily. Starlite runs 5–7pm daily. Fort Oak runs 4–6pm Tue–Sun. Baja Betty's also offers an extended happy hour from 6pm to close.

    What's the best bar in Hillcrest for happy hour?

    Hillcrest Brewing Company is the neighbourhood anchor — daily 2–6pm happy hour with $2 off drafts, wines, and wells on a covered patio. Baja Betty's is the best value if you want cocktails: Papi Hour runs 2–6pm and extended happy hour continues 6pm to close with $6.75 margaritas, sangria, and well drinks.

    Is Hillcrest good for a happy hour crawl?

    Yes. University Avenue and 5th Avenue have enough walkable options to build a full crawl. Start at Hillcrest Brewing at 2pm, hit Common Stock and Baja Betty's on University, then finish at Starlite or Fort Oak as the evening picks up. All within 15 minutes on foot.

    Does Hillcrest have happy hours on weekends?

    Yes. Hillcrest Brewing Company runs happy hour daily including weekends (2–6pm). Baja Betty's Papi Hour is also daily (2–6pm, plus extended 6pm–close). Starlite runs happy hour daily 5–7pm. Fort Oak runs happy hour Tuesday through Sunday.
