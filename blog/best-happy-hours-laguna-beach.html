Best Happy Hours in Laguna Beach (2026)

From The Cliff's oceanfront fire pit to Coyote Grill's legendary margaritas and Mozambique's rooftop — every verified Laguna Beach happy hour worth your time.

By Leah June 5, 2026 8 min read
Share

Laguna Beach does a lot of things beautifully — the art galleries, the limestone coves, the way the light hits the Pacific Coast Highway right around 5pm. One thing it doesn't always do cheaply: drinking. But the best happy hours in Laguna Beach are genuinely some of the most scenic deals in all of Southern California, and if you know the right spots and the right windows, you can nurse a cocktail with an ocean view for the price of a suburban bar tab.

Best Happy Hours in Laguna Beach: The Verified List

The Cliff Restaurant — Best Oceanfront Patio

Happy hour Monday–Friday, 3–6pm — Half-off all appetizers; $22 off beverages in the bar and fire pit area.

The Cliff Restaurant at 577 S Coast Hwy is the anchor of any honest Laguna Beach happy hour conversation. The view from the outdoor fire pit patio is one of the finest in coastal Southern California — a clean drop to the water, no obstruction, the kind of vista that's been on Instagram feeds for years. Half-off appetizers during the 3–6pm window applies to a menu that includes really good calamari, fish tacos, and raw bar items. The $22 off beverages discount is applied at the bar area and fire pit, making cocktails that are normally $16+ suddenly reasonable.

It fills early on Fridays — arrive by 3:30pm if you want an outdoor table. The weekday-only restriction (Monday–Friday) is the one drawback; plan accordingly if your weekend trip to Laguna overlaps.

Coyote Grill — Best Daily Happy Hour

Happy hour daily, 3–6pm — House margaritas, domestic and Mexican beer, well drinks, shot of the week at special pricing; food menu includes calamari tacos, crab ceviche, bacon wrapped shrimp.

If there's one venue that defines the Laguna Beach happy hour scene for locals, it's Coyote Grill at 31621 Coast Hwy. Running seven days a week (the only major oceanview spot that does), the daily 3–6pm window draws a mix of regulars and visitors who've learned the secret. The calamari taco — voted Laguna's #1 taco by Best of Laguna Beach readers for multiple years running — is the order. The margaritas are everything you'd expect from a Mexican restaurant on the Pacific Coast Highway. The crab ceviche is the underrated move. The food pricing during happy hour is genuine value for the location.

This is the place to go on a Saturday afternoon when everyone else's happy hour is closed or restricted. Outdoor seating has PCH views. It gets lively but never feels like the wrong crowd.

Mozambique — Best Rooftop Views

Happy hour Monday–Friday 3–6pm, Saturday–Sunday 3–5pm — $5 all draft beers, $5 well cocktails, $6 mojito, $6 house margarita, $5 featured wines; food plates from $7.

Mozambique at 1740 S Coast Hwy is a three-story venue with a concept that shouldn't work as well as it does — South African coastal cuisine on a California beach — and yet it's become one of the most genuinely beloved happy hour spots in Laguna. The Veranda rooftop on the upper floor has panoramic views up and down the coast that rival The Cliff. The pricing is some of the most aggressive in Laguna for the quality: $5 drafts, $5 well cocktails, $6 margaritas. The weekend happy hour (3–5pm Saturday–Sunday) is shorter than the weekday window but makes Mozambique one of the only real options for a scenic, affordable Saturday drink before dinner.

Order the peri-peri prawns or the chicken skewers from the happy hour food menu — the South African spice profile is genuinely different from anything else on PCH.

Starfish — Best Happy Hour for Food

Happy hour daily, 3–6pm — 30+ small plates and sushi rolls from $11; discounted sake, wine, beer, and specialty libations.

If you're going to Laguna Beach for the food and the happy hour is the delivery mechanism, Starfish at 30832 S Pacific Coast Hwy is your best move. Their "Opium Hour" (the house name for 3–6pm daily) covers an extraordinary menu: over 30 small plates drawing from Thailand, Vietnam, Korea, and Japan — fresh sushi rolls, crispy wontons, wok-fired proteins, curries. It's one of the most ambitious happy hour food menus in Orange County, and it runs every single day of the week. The specialty libations include house cocktails and sake options curated specifically for the menu.

The room is beautiful — warm lighting, clean modern design, great for a date or a small group. The daily availability makes it a reliable fallback any day of the week.

Lumberyard — Best Downtown Location

Happy hour Monday–Friday, 3–6pm (bar and patio only) — $5 beer, $8 wine, $8 cocktails; food $10 includes fish tacos, chicken lollipops, zucchini planks, mezze plate, portobello mushroom frites.

Lumberyard at 384 Forest Ave sits in the historic Lumberyard building in the heart of downtown Laguna — blocks from the beach, surrounded by galleries and boutiques. It's the most walkable happy hour on this list, which matters if you're spending the afternoon in the village and don't want to drive for a drink before dinner. The $8 flat pricing on both wine and cocktails is clean and fair; the food $10 flat is some of the best deal value per plate in Laguna. The chicken lollipops are the move. The patio is open-air and faces Forest Ave, giving you front-row access to Laguna's perpetual people-watching.

Bar and patio only for the happy hour pricing — the dining room operates at standard rates during the same window.

Hennessey's Tavern — Best Late Happy Hour

Happy hour Monday–Friday, 4–7pm — $5 happy hour drinks, $6 happy hour appetizers.

Hennessey's Tavern at 213 Ocean Ave is the most important detail on this list for anyone who can't arrive before 6pm: it runs until 7pm. That's a full hour longer than every other option here, which for the working crowd or anyone arriving on a late Amtrak from LA is the difference between catching a deal or not. Positioned on Ocean Ave close to the beach, the tavern has that lived-in Irish pub energy that Laguna doesn't always have. $5 drinks is the flattest and most predictable pricing on the list. $6 apps covers a menu that includes the usual suspects done well.

The Monday–Friday restriction applies, so this is a weekday-only option. But as the latest standard happy hour in Laguna Beach, it earns its place on every locals' shortlist.

When to Go: A Laguna Beach Happy Hour Cheat Sheet

The honest truth about Laguna Beach happy hours is that most of them run 3–6pm on weekdays, which is the standard Southern California window. If you're there on a Thursday or Friday, you have six solid options with overlapping windows and can plan an informal bar crawl down PCH. Weekends compress quickly: only Coyote Grill (daily) and Mozambique (Saturday–Sunday 3–5pm) give you real options on Saturday or Sunday afternoon. And if you arrive late on a weekday, Hennessey's is your safety net at 4–7pm.

One practical note: Laguna Beach parking is limited and PCH traffic backs up significantly after 4pm in summer. If you're going to The Cliff or Mozambique on a weekday, arrive closer to 3pm when parking is more available and tables are easier to get. The downtown spots (Lumberyard, Hennessey's) are walkable from the village parking structure on Forest Ave.

Beyond Happy Hour: What Else to Do in Laguna Beach

If you're making a day of it, Laguna Beach rewards time. The Main Beach boardwalk is genuinely worth the visit — a broad stretch of sand with the Craftsman-style lifeguard towers that have appeared in more Instagram carousels than you can count. The Laguna Beach Arts District along PCH and in the village has over 70 galleries, mostly walkable. The tide pools at Crystal Cove State Park to the north are some of the best accessible tide pools in Southern California. Combined with a 3–6pm happy hour window at any of the spots above, you have a complete late afternoon and evening without spending much.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best happy hour in Laguna Beach?
The best overall happy hour in Laguna Beach is The Cliff Restaurant for its unbeatable combination of oceanfront views and half-off appetizers daily 3–6pm Monday–Friday. For daily availability including weekends, Coyote Grill and Starfish both run happy hour seven days a week from 3–6pm.
Which Laguna Beach bars have happy hour on weekends?
Coyote Grill and Starfish both offer happy hour seven days a week from 3–6pm, making them the top choices for weekend happy hours in Laguna Beach. Mozambique also runs a weekend happy hour Saturday–Sunday from 3–5pm. Most other Laguna Beach happy hours are weekday-only (Monday–Friday).
What time does happy hour end in Laguna Beach?
Most Laguna Beach happy hours end at 6pm. The Cliff, Coyote Grill, Lumberyard, and Starfish all run 3–6pm. Hennessey's Tavern is the exception — their happy hour runs until 7pm, Monday–Friday 4–7pm, giving you an extra hour after the standard window closes.
Where can I find oceanview happy hours in Laguna Beach?
The Cliff Restaurant (577 S Coast Hwy) and Mozambique (1740 S Coast Hwy) are the two standout oceanview happy hours in Laguna Beach. The Cliff has a direct fire pit patio facing the water; Mozambique's Veranda rooftop offers panoramic views up and down the coast. Both run Monday–Friday 3–6pm.
