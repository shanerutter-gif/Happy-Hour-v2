Best Happy Hours in Newport Beach (2026)

Waterfront deals at Tavern House, $5 street tacos at SOL, Nobu sushi specials, and Starfish’s legendary Opium Hour — the best Newport Beach happy hour spots, verified.

By Shane May 29, 2026 8 min read
Share
📋 In this article

    Newport Beach happy hour is its own category. You're not hunting for a deal in a dark dive bar — you're watching sailboats drift past the harbor while someone refills your glass of house red for $10. The deals exist. You just need to know where to look.

    We went through the full OC happy hour landscape to find the spots that actually deliver — on price, on vibe, and on the thing Newport does better than anywhere: the view. Every venue on this list was verified open and running their current deals as of May 2026.

    What Makes Newport Beach Happy Hour Different

    Newport Beach operates at a different baseline than the rest of Southern California. The restaurants are nicer, the real estate is pricier, and the clientele generally isn't searching for two-dollar tacos. That creates an interesting opportunity: happy hour here often includes things that would be premium elsewhere.

    A few things that set Newport's scene apart:

    • Waterfront access is everywhere. Newport Harbor, the bay, PCH overlooks — genuinely good views are part of the deal, not an upgrade.
    • Happy hour food is real food. We're talking grilled sweet corn, firecracker quesadillas, freshly rolled sushi, and market-price raw bar items, all discounted.
    • Weekday happy hours run long. Most spots give you 3–4 hours of window, which is generous compared to the aggressive 4–6pm squeeze you see in LA.
    • The dress code is relaxed. Boat shoes and a light jacket is Newport's version of casual Friday. You won't feel underdressed, and you won't feel overdressed either.

    Best Waterfront Happy Hour — Tavern House Kitchen + Bar

    If you picture Newport Beach happy hour, this is probably what you're imagining. Tavern House sits right on the harbor at 333 Bayside Drive, and the patio looks directly onto the water. Sailboats, pelicans, the whole thing.

    The deals

    Happy hour daily 4–6pm — House cocktails, draft beers, and select wines all at discounted prices, plus a stripped-back bar menu of appetizers and small bites. The bar area fills quickly after 4:30, especially Thursday through Saturday, so show up closer to opening if you want a patio table with an unobstructed harbor view.

    The food leans New American — think charcuterie, elevated bar snacks, and wood-fired options. Nothing at Tavern House is "cheap" in the sense that a PB dive bar is cheap, but the ambiance means you're getting way more than you're paying for at happy hour prices. This is a date night spot that doesn't require a special occasion budget.

    333 Bayside Dr, Newport Beach · Daily 4–6pm · (949) 673-8464

    Best Mexican + Margs — SOL Mexican Cocina

    SOL sits right on Pacific Coast Highway with patio seating that practically hangs over the water. The vibe is upscale coastal Mexican — think Ensenada meets Malibu — and the happy hour deals are genuinely some of the best value in Newport.

    The deals

    Happy hour Mon–Fri 3–6pm (Cantina and Patio only) — House margaritas, watermelon margaritas, and well cocktails all at the $5–8 range. On the food side: $5 grilled sweet corn, $6 guacamole, $7 firecracker quesadilla, and street tacos running $4–5 each. You can eat and drink well here for $25–30 per person, which for the location and quality is hard to beat in Newport.

    SOL gets legitimately busy on Friday afternoons — locals start filtering in around 3:30pm. Midweek is more relaxed. Either way, the patio seats on the Cantina side get the best breeze off the water.

    251 Pacific Coast Hwy, Newport Beach · Mon–Fri 3–6pm

    Best for Splurging (a Little) — Nobu Newport Beach

    Nobu on a budget sounds contradictory. It isn't. Nobu Newport Beach runs one of the most surprisingly accessible happy hours in the area — and it's the kind of thing you can bring someone to without explaining that you're technically being frugal.

    The deals

    Happy hour Mon–Thu 5–7pm — Cold dishes under $14, sushi maki rolls under $10, hot dishes under $16. Japanese beers under $8, sake at $11, cocktails from $14–16. The yellow tail sashimi and rock shrimp tempura are the moves. A couple of cocktails and a round of maki clocks in around $40–50 per person, which for Nobu is essentially free.

    The location — at the Lido Marina Village on Via Oporto — is walking distance from the water and has the low-lit, design-forward interior you'd expect from the brand. Reservations are a good idea for Mon–Thu; it's popular with locals who know the happy hour trick.

    3450 Via Oporto, Newport Beach · Mon–Thu 5–7pm

    Best Laguna Beach Option — Starfish (Opium Hour)

    About 20 minutes south of Newport on PCH, Starfish in Laguna Beach runs what they call "Opium Hour" — a name that's a little on the nose but accurately describes what sitting on their terrace watching the Pacific does to you.

    The deals

    Happy hour (Opium Hour) daily 3–6pm — Over 30 small plates and rolls starting at $11, discounted sake, beer, wine, and their signature house cocktails. Starfish serves Asian coastal cuisine — the lobster tempura roll and the hamachi crudo both appear on the happy hour menu at prices significantly below what you'd pay at dinner. The sake selection is serious for a Southern California beach bar.

    This one works best as a destination. Make it the anchor of an afternoon on PCH rather than a quick after-work stop — the drive from Newport is genuinely scenic and Laguna Beach as a whole is worth the detour.

    30832 S Pacific Coast Hwy, Laguna Beach · Daily 3–6pm · (949) 715-9200

    Tips for Newport Beach Happy Hour

    A few things that are obvious once you've been here a few times but less obvious the first time:

    • Traffic is real on PCH. The 3pm happy hours sound great until you're sitting in beachside gridlock at 2:45. If you're coming from anywhere in LA, budget 30–40 extra minutes on a weekday afternoon.
    • Parking is metered near the harbor. The Lido Marina Village area has a few nearby garages. Tavern House and SOL both have some lot parking, but it fills by 5pm on busy days. Get there early or park a few blocks inland and walk.
    • Weekends change the math. SOL's happy hour is weekdays only. Tavern House runs daily. Starfish runs daily. Nobu is Mon–Thu only. If you're planning a weekend Newport bar crawl, check the weekday-specific deals don't apply.
    • Combine Laguna with Newport on the same trip. Hit SOL or Tavern House at 3pm in Newport, drive the PCH south, and catch Starfish's Opium Hour before it ends at 6. You'll spend about $40 per person total and cover 25 miles of some of the best coastline in California.

    Newport Beach vs. San Diego Happy Hour

    We cover San Diego extensively on Spotd — it's our home market and has one of the deepest happy hour scenes in the country. Newport is a different animal. The floor is higher (you're not finding $2.50 beers on the harbor), but the experience ceiling is also different. Newport at its best — a $10 glass of wine on a harbor patio at golden hour — is hard to replicate anywhere in San Diego.

    If you're already in OC or making a day trip, these four stops cover the best of what Newport Beach happy hour has to offer right now. No bad seats, no tourist traps, and no deals that turn out to be significantly worse than advertised once you get there.
