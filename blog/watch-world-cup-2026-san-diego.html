Where to Watch the 2026 World Cup in San Diego

The FIFA World Cup is here — and San Diego's Gaslamp Quarter is turning into a proper soccer city. Here's where to watch every match, which bars are running drink deals during game time, and what to expect at each spot.

By Tyler June 14, 2026 7 min read
Share

The 2026 FIFA World Cup started June 11. San Diego might not be a host city, but where to watch the World Cup in San Diego is one of the more interesting questions in town right now — because the Gaslamp Quarter and downtown core have been building out their sports bar infrastructure for years, and they are ready. The group stage schedule through July runs afternoon and evening matches on Pacific Time that land almost perfectly inside happy hour windows, which means the 2026 tournament is one of the rare sporting events where “watching the game” and “getting a drink deal” are the same activity.

This guide covers seven verified San Diego bars showing the World Cup in 2026. Every venue has been checked for Saturday availability, screen quality, and drink deals that apply during game time. Use Spotd to see which of these spots are active tonight and what their current happy hour status is.

Best Bars to Watch the 2026 World Cup in San Diego

Tom’s Watch Bar — Best Overall World Cup Experience

Open Saturday 9am–2am · No Saturday happy hour (weekday social hour 3–6pm Mon–Fri)

Tom’s Watch Bar at 815 J St in the Gaslamp is the closest thing San Diego has to a purpose-built sports viewing venue, and the 2026 World Cup is exactly what it was built for. Every wall is a screen. The audio follows each match. VIP Matchday Seating gives larger groups a reserved table with a dedicated sightline for major games — a detail that matters during knockout rounds when the bar fills up before kickoff.

Tom’s is promoting this explicitly as “The World's Biggest Soccer Party” and it has the infrastructure to back that up. The downside is that weekday social hour pricing (3–6pm, Monday through Friday) does not extend to Saturday, so if you are here for a weekend match you are paying full price on drinks. For the viewing experience alone, it is the correct call for USA matches and any knockout-round game worth reserving a table for.

Rustic Root — Best Saturday Happy Hour + World Cup Combo

Saturday happy hour 3–5pm: half-off draft beers · $6 well spirits · $8 featured wines · $9 cocktails

Rustic Root at 535 Fifth Ave is the most important venue on this list for anyone watching World Cup games on a Saturday. The rooftop Gaslamp bar runs a genuine Saturday happy hour from 3–5pm with half-off draft beers, $6 well spirits, $8 featured wines, and $9 cocktails — deals that apply to the extensive cocktail list, not just the bottom-shelf options. The Saturday 3pm kickoff window maps directly onto the typical afternoon group stage match (which air at 1pm and 4pm PT), making this the best value option in the Gaslamp for game-day drinking.

Rustic Root has multiple screens and a strong draft selection, and the rooftop patio is one of the better outdoor watching setups in the Gaslamp. For a Saturday afternoon where you want discounted cocktails and a relaxed rooftop setting rather than a packed sports bar, this is the call. Happy hour appetizers during the window include Parmesan Fries at $6.75 and the Rustic Smashburger at $12.75.

Garage Kitchen + Bar — Best World Cup Watch Party Setup

Open Friday–Sunday from 11am · World Cup watch party with game-day specials

Garage Kitchen + Bar in downtown San Diego has set up a dedicated World Cup watch party configuration with giant screens, discounted parking, and a game-day food menu that runs alongside the matches. The Friday–Sunday 11am open time makes it viable for the morning group stage matches that air at 10am PT, which most bars in the city are not open for.

The watch party format includes bottomless mimosas for weekend brunch, which makes the earlier matches a different kind of sports bar experience than the evening-focused options in the Gaslamp. For groups who want the full game-day setup — reserved space, giant screen, food and drink package — Garage Kitchen + Bar is worth checking directly for current package availability.

Hidden Craft — Best Craft Beer Option for Every Game

Open Saturday 11am–9pm · Streaming every group stage and knockout match

Hidden Craft at 600 W Broadway in downtown San Diego is streaming every 2026 World Cup match — not just the marquee games — which makes it the right choice for fans who want to catch a specific group stage fixture that the bigger bars might not prioritize. The venue has 30+ self-serve taps from local San Diego craft breweries, a private patio, and a family-friendly, pet-friendly atmosphere that makes it a different kind of sports venue than the Gaslamp alternatives.

The self-serve tap system means you pour your own beer and pay by the ounce, which keeps the pace fast during matches and eliminates the wait for service during big moments. Saturday hours of 11am–9pm cover the morning and afternoon match windows well. For craft beer drinkers who want to watch a Tuesday Sweden vs. Tunisia game or a group stage match that has not gotten mainstream coverage, Hidden Craft is the dependable option downtown.

Siamo Napoli — Best Happy Hour During Evening Kickoffs

Daily happy hour 4–6pm at the bar (including Saturdays & Sundays) · $5 wines · discounted pizza and appetizers

Siamo Napoli at 3959 30th St in North Park is a MICHELIN Guide restaurant with a bar happy hour that runs seven days a week from 4–6pm. The 4pm Saturday and Sunday window is worth noting specifically: it overlaps with the afternoon match window (typically kicking off around 4pm PT), and the bar-only happy hour pricing gives you $5 wines and discounted Neapolitan pizza during game time without the full restaurant pricing structure. There is also a 9–11pm happy hour on Fridays and Saturdays for late matches.

The venue is smaller and more neighborhood-local than the Gaslamp options, with a screen setup oriented toward a bar crowd rather than a stadium atmosphere. If you are in North Park and want to catch a 4pm or 7pm match over genuinely good wood-fired pizza and wine priced at $5 a glass, this is one of the better-value combinations in San Diego during the tournament.

Postino Little Italy — Best Wine Bar Option

Daily happy hour until 5pm · $7 glasses of wine · $7 pitchers of beer

Postino Little Italy at 2100 Kettner Blvd runs daily happy hour pricing until 5pm every day of the week, including Saturdays and Sundays. The $7 wine glass and $7 pitcher pricing applies across a solid wine list and catches the 1pm and afternoon group stage matches within the window. Postino is not a dedicated sports bar — the atmosphere is more wine-bar-casual than game-day — but the screens are present and the daily happy hour structure makes it the most consistent value on this list for the mornings and early afternoons.

For groups watching a quieter group stage match where you want good wine over the game rather than a bar experience built around it, the combination of daily pricing and the Little Italy location (walkable from downtown) makes Postino a practical choice. The $30 bruschetta board and bottle deal on Monday and Tuesday evenings after 8pm is also worth noting for late-match viewing nights.

Barleymash — Best Gaslamp Atmosphere for Big Matches

Open daily · Weekday happy hour Mon–Fri 3–5pm (half-off all alcohol) · Giant screens + full sound

Barleymash at 600 5th Ave in the Gaslamp does not run a Saturday happy hour, but for weekday matches — and the World Cup group stage runs Monday through Friday as well — the Monday–Friday 3–5pm half-off all alcohol deal is one of the best drink specials in the Gaslamp. During USA matches or any high-profile fixture, the combination of Barleymash’s giant screen setup, full match audio, and the Gaslamp crowd makes it an energized environment for the games that matter.

For evening matches on weekdays when you want a Gaslamp bar with a real crowd and full stadium sound, Barleymash is worth being at. The bar is listed on the official Gaslamp Quarter World Cup venue guide, and the management has been promoting specifically for big matches throughout the tournament.

How to Plan Your World Cup Bar Schedule in San Diego

The 2026 group stage match schedule in Pacific Time runs across four windows: roughly 10am, 1pm, 4pm, and 7pm PT depending on the day. The afternoon windows (1pm and 4pm PT) are where the happy hour math works best. Rustic Root’s Saturday 3–5pm window directly overlaps the afternoon match. Siamo Napoli’s daily 4–6pm window catches the second afternoon kickoff. Postino’s daily-until-5pm deal covers the 1pm window comfortably.

For morning games (10am PT), Hidden Craft opens at 11am Saturday and catches the tail end. Garage Kitchen + Bar opens at 11am Friday through Sunday and is one of the few spots in San Diego genuinely set up for the early matches with food and drink options. For weekday happy hour during group stage games, Barleymash’s 3–5pm half-off window and Tom’s Watch Bar’s 3–6pm weekday social hour are the primary options in the Gaslamp.
