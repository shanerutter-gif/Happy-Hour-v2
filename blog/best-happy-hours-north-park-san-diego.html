Best Happy Hours in North Park, San Diego (2026)

From craft cocktails at Polite Provisions to $6 bourbon at Seven Grand — North Park's 30th Street corridor is the best happy hour stretch in San Diego. Here's every deal, verified.

By Shane May 30, 2026 9 min read
Share
📋 In this article

    Ask a local where to go for happy hour in San Diego and nine times out of ten they'll say North Park. The stretch of 30th Street between University and Adams has more craft beer taprooms, cocktail bars, and wine-forward restaurants per block than anywhere else in the city — and most of them run genuine happy hour deals that actually move the needle on your tab.

    This isn't a neighborhood where happy hour means a $1 off well drinks and sad nachos. North Park happy hour means $6 hand-crafted cocktails from James Beard-nominated bartenders, $2 off pints at breweries that win medals at GABF, and a patio culture where the 4 PM crowd is as serious about their drinks as the 11 PM crowd.

    We combed every bar on the 30th Street corridor — and the surrounding streets — to pull together every active deal. Here's the full North Park happy hour map, organized by what you're actually looking for.

    Why North Park Wins for Happy Hour

    Three things set North Park apart from every other San Diego neighborhood when it comes to happy hour:

    • The density is unreal. You can start at one end of 30th Street and bar-hop through five different happy hours without moving your car. Polite Provisions, Seven Grand, Coin-Op, The Office Bar, Crazee Burger — they're all within a five-minute walk of each other.
    • The quality ceiling is higher. This isn't tourist-trap pricing with a "happy hour" sticker on it. The cocktails at Polite Provisions that cost $6 during happy hour are better than most $18 cocktails downtown.
    • The windows run late. Pacific Beach happy hours end at 6pm. North Park has spots running until 8pm, 9pm, and even midnight. You can show up after work and still catch a deal.

    Best Cocktail Bars

    Polite Provisions

    Happy hour Mon–Fri open until 6:30pm — Polite Provisions is the reason people move to North Park. James Beard-nominated bartenders, a menu that reads more like a novel than a drinks list, and a space that somehow feels both grand and cozy. During happy hour the pricing drops enough to make it your regular spot instead of your splurge. The classic cocktails are where to start — the Gimlet and the Mezcal Negroni are standards for a reason. Address: 4696 30th St.

    Seven Grand San Diego

    Happy hour daily 4–8pm — $6 draft beers, select bourbon and Irish whiskey; $8 featured classic cocktails. Seven Grand is what a whiskey bar is supposed to feel like: low light, pool tables, live music on weekends, and a back bar with more bourbon labels than you have time to work through. The 4-hour happy hour window is one of the longest in North Park, and the $6 whiskey spec is genuinely one of the best deals in the neighborhood. Address: 3054 University Ave.

    The Smoking Goat

    Happy hour daily 3–7pm — $6 select draft beers and Japanese Highball; $8 cocktails including the signature Smiles for Margs, Prickle & Chill, and Mai Tai; $10 Espresso Martini; $30 Disco Punch Bowl (serves 4). Four-hour window, solid food deals ($5.26 battered fries, $6.43 house salad, $8.49 pretzel sticks), and the Disco Punch Bowl is the move if you're coming with a group. Address: 3408 30th St.

    Best Craft Beer Taprooms

    North Park Beer Company

    Happy hour Mon–Fri 3–6pm — $2 off all pints during happy hour, plus live music on weekends and a dog-friendly taproom. North Park Beer Co. has been anchoring the University Ave beer scene for years with consistent, award-caliber brewing. The $2 off pints isn't a massive discount in absolute terms, but when you're starting from a taproom pour on house-brewed IPA, it makes the afternoon very affordable. Address: 3038 University Ave.

    Fall Brewing North Park

    Pints from $6, open late Fri–Sat — Fall Brewing is the kind of neighborhood taproom that San Diego does better than anywhere in the country: a narrow space on 30th, a rotating tap list of approachable lagers, IPAs, and seasonals, and a crowd that's equally mixed between regulars and first-timers. No pretension. Good beer. The Friday night window stretches to midnight if you're not ready for the evening to end. Address: 4542 30th St.

    Best for Food + Drinks

    Crazee Burger

    Happy hour daily 3:10–6:19pm — $5.99 draught beers, $6.99 wine, $3.99 Crazee fries, $4.49 sweet potato fries, $4.99 frings, $8.99 signature burger. Crazee Burger's happy hour window is oddly specific (3:10 to 6:19) but the deals are real. This is the go-to for when you want actual food with your drinks — the burger + beer combo around $15 total is hard to beat anywhere on the street. Google rating: 4.6. Address: 3993 30th St.

    Caffè Calabria

    Happy hour Mon–Fri 4–6pm — $5 house wine and select beers; $6 Aperol Spritz and Vodka Collins; $7 Sbagliato, Negroni, and Old Fashioned; $12 pizzas. An Italian coffee bar by day, an aperitivo bar by evening — and the 4–6pm window is when it really shines. The $7 Negroni is exceptional. The $12 Neapolitan-style pizzas make this a full dinner option. If you want to feel like you're in a piazza in Naples for two hours, this is your spot. Google rating: 4.6. Address: 3933 30th St Suite 2.

    Coin-Op Game Room

    Happy hour daily 5–7pm — $5 select drafts, $6 wells, $7 classic cocktails, $2 cheese slices, $10 cheese and pepperoni pies. Coin-Op earns its spot on this list not just for the deals but for the vibe: dozens of vintage arcade games, pinball machines, and a crowd that's genuinely having a good time. The $5 draft + $2 cheese slice combo is basically the adult version of a happy meal. Google rating: 4.4. Address: 3926 30th St.

    Best Late-Night Happy Hour

    The Office Bar

    Happy hour daily 5–9pm — $1 off wine, bottles, cans, and non-alcoholic drinks; $2 off all drafts and wells; $3 off signature cocktails. The Office Bar runs the longest dedicated happy hour window on 30th Street — a full four hours, ending at 9pm when most North Park happy hours have been closed for two hours. This is your spot if you can't make 6pm but still want a deal. The space is a genuine neighborhood local: no pretension, solid pours, and a crowd that's there to drink, not be seen. Google rating: 4.1. Address: 3936 30th St.

    West Coast Tavern

    Happy hour + late night 11pm–12am — $5 well drinks, select drafts, and house wine; $7 select specialty cocktails; $20 mule or donkey pitchers; $7 wings, hummus, bratwurst, waffle fries, and sliders. West Coast Tavern is a two-fer: a standard early happy hour and a late-night happy hour from 11pm to midnight. If your Friday stretches longer than planned, the 11pm window is a rare find. Open midnight daily. Address: 2895 University Ave.

    Best Cheap Night Out

    Working Class

    North Park's favorite no-frills neighborhood bar, with $5 draft beers, $6 wine, and $2 off all starters. Working Class replaced Ritual Tavern after its decade-long run and has carved out its own identity as the place where the neighborhood actually drinks — not the place it Instagrams. The patio fills up early on weeknights, which tells you everything you need to know. Address: 30th St corridor.

    Good News Bar

    $4 wells and domestics, $5 craft beers, no-cover dive bar pricing all day. Good News Bar is exactly what the name implies. No deals that expire at 6pm, no minimums, no attitude. If you want the cheapest drinks on the strip without a happy hour clock stressing you out, this is it. Address: 3955 30th St.

    How to Plan Your North Park Happy Hour Crawl

    The 30th Street corridor is walkable end-to-end in about 20 minutes, which means a multi-stop happy hour crawl is genuinely doable without a rideshare. A few routes that work well:

    • The Aperitivo Route (4–7pm): Start at Caffè Calabria for a $7 Negroni and $12 pizza. Walk north to The Smoking Goat for a second round. Finish at Seven Grand for $6 bourbon and a pool table.
    • The Craft Beer Loop (3–6pm): North Park Beer Co. for house-brewed pints, then south to Fall Brewing, then east to Crazee Burger for food. All within a 10-minute walk loop.
    • The Night Owl Route (5–9pm+): Start at Coin-Op for $5 drafts and arcade games at 5pm. Move to The Office Bar when Coin-Op ends at 7pm — you'll catch two more hours of deals. West Coast Tavern's 11pm–midnight window is your finale.
